# Buyer Personas

---

## Primary: Head of Sales / VP Sales

The most common buyer at companies matching our [[icp]].

- **Title:** VP Sales, Head of Sales, CRO (at smaller companies)
- **Pain:** Their AEs spend 30-60 minutes per account on manual research before calls. Top performers do this well; average performers skip it or do it poorly. The VP wants to raise the floor.
- **Motivation:** Pipeline efficiency, win rates, deal velocity. Wants reps spending time selling, not researching.
- **Buying behavior:** Can make unilateral purchasing decisions for tools under $50K. Values demos that show immediate applicability to their team's workflow.
- **Adoption path:** Sees the demo, gets excited, asks "can my team use this tomorrow?"
- **Risk:** As Borys from ClickUp noted, VP Sales can be protective of their team's leads and skeptical of tools that threaten the human sales motion. Position as augmentation, not replacement.

---

## Secondary: Technical Founder Doing Founder-Led Sales

Common at Series A-B companies where the founder is still the primary seller.

- **Title:** CEO, CTO, Co-founder (doing sales themselves)
- **Pain:** They're engineers who hate manual research but need to prepare deeply for high-stakes meetings. They over-index on product knowledge and under-index on buyer knowledge.
- **Motivation:** Wants to sell like a pro without learning to be a salesperson. Agent-native tools feel natural to them because they already live in Claude Code / Cursor.
- **Buying behavior:** Fast decision-maker. If the demo works, they'll buy or adopt immediately. No procurement process.
- **Adoption path:** Tries it in Claude Code themselves, sees the value, starts using it daily for their own outbound.
- **Risk:** May outgrow founder-led sales and hire a VP Sales who has their own tool preferences.

---

## Tertiary: Chief of Staff / "Founder's Office" Operator / GTM Engineer

Emerging role at fast-growing startups. The person who builds strategic systems for the CEO.

- **Title:** Chief of Staff, Head of Strategy, Founder's Office, GTM Engineer
- **Pain:** Tasked with building GTM systems rapidly for a company growing faster than its processes. Needs to set up prospecting, research, and outbound infrastructure in weeks, not months.
- **Motivation:** Efficiency and speed. Wants to plug intelligence into existing agent workflows (Gumloop, Dust, Slack bots) rather than adopt another standalone tool.
- **Buying behavior:** Evaluates on integration ease and data quality. Cares deeply about "does it work with what we already have?"
- **Adoption path:** Wants API key, plugs into existing agent infrastructure, evaluates data quality on their specific targets.
- **Example:** Daniel Liem at Decagon -- building a GTM engineering team, already using Gumloop/Dust, wanted to plug Eventual into their Slack bot workflow. Immediately asked "how are you getting this data?" -- cared about the pipeline, not just the output.
- **Risk:** May be the champion but not the budget holder. Need to navigate to the VP Sales or CEO for final approval.
