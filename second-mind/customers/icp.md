# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> This is the canonical ICP definition for the Eventual Entity Search era (Feb 2026 onward). It supersedes the earlier product-icp.md from the search sprint era.

---

## Core ICP Criteria (Deliberately Narrow)

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Industry** | AI/ML companies |
| **Stage** | Series A-D (including "rocketship" companies at Series D+ with high funding velocity) |
| **Size** | 50-500 employees |
| **Outbound team** | 3+ outbound reps |
| **GTM motion** | Outbound-heavy |
| **Deal size** | $30K+ ACV |
| **Engineering culture** | Agent-forward (already using Claude Code / Cursor) |

This is a beachhead, not a destination.

---

## Estimated Addressable Pool

**200-500 companies globally** that match all core criteria.

This is intentionally small. We are not trying to serve the entire market. We are trying to find 10 people who would be genuinely unhappy if we took the tool away.

---

## Expansion Path

| Step | Trigger | Addressable Pool |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| **Step 1: Go upmarket + adjacent verticals** | 10+ AI company logos secured | 2,000-5,000 companies |
| **Step 2: Drop agent-forward requirement** | Product proves value with non-technical users | 10,000-20,000 companies |
| **Step 3: New domains** (recruiting, real estate, investment research) | Data flywheel working, infrastructure generalizes | TAM expands dramatically |

## Why These Criteria

### AI/ML companies
- Engineering culture means they already use Claude Code / Cursor -- adoption friction is minimal
- They understand MCP and agent-native interfaces
- We know this ecosystem and can leverage our network

### Series A-D, 50-500 employees
- Large enough to have a real outbound motion with dedicated reps
- Small enough that buying decisions are fast (no 6-month procurement cycles)
- Growing fast enough that GTM efficiency matters urgently

### 3+ outbound reps, outbound-heavy
- Research pain is proportional to outbound volume -- more calls = more prep time = more pain
- Outbound-heavy means they're actively seeking target accounts (vs. inbound-led)

### $30K+ ACV
- High ACV = account-based selling = deep research is worth the investment
- Spray-and-pray doesn't need deep intelligence; account-based selling does

### Agent-forward
- Not trying to convince skeptics -- selling to believers
- Already have the infrastructure (Claude Code, agent frameworks) to consume our product
- Reduces adoption friction to near zero

## ICP's Targets (Critical for Data Strategy)

Our ICP is startups selling upmarket. They're researching their buyers (Series C to Fortune 500), not other startups. This is the core of [[hypotheses]] H3:

- Decagon targets Fortune 1000 (healthcare focus)
- ClickUp sells to enterprise
- Typical target: mid-market to Fortune 500 in tech-adjacent verticals

This means our data strategy must prioritize deep coverage of mid-market/enterprise -- not just the AI/ML ecosystem we know well. See [[data-strategy]] for details.
