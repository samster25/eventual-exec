## Pain Points Identified

- **Top of funnel is broken** — outbound volume steady but reply rates and meetings booked declining. Not enough pipeline to hit targets.
- **No SDRs** — AEs do their own prospecting AND serve as CSMs. Time is split across too many functions.
- **Can't surface "have they failed at AI?"** — the most important qualification signal isn't available through Gemini or ZoomInfo. Currently only discoverable in live conversation.
- **Events are a waste of money** — great conversations on-site, zero follow-through after.
- **Competing with free** — Microsoft Copilot, Google, Anthropic announcements can kill deals at the finish line (Patrick Industries example).
- **ZoomInfo intent signals not accurate enough** — shows who's looking but not what they've actually done or tried.

## Key Learnings

1. **"Have they failed at AI?" is a killer qualification signal.** Kyle would pay more than ZoomInfo for this intelligence. Gemini Deep Research can't surface it. This is a data asset Eventual could potentially build — tracking companies' AI adoption attempts, vendor relationships, and failure patterns.
2. **Outbound is broken industry-wide.** Constant volume, declining reply rates. The shift is toward higher-intent, higher-quality engagement. AI has commoditized personalization but hasn't solved the "getting attention" problem.
3. **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is becoming a real inbound channel.** Hyperscience is getting inbound leads who found them through ChatGPT recommendations. Marketing teams are starting to optimize for LLM mentions.

**Surprises:**
- The "have they failed?" qualification filter — they actively disqualify prospects who haven't failed at AI yet.
- ChatGPT as a lead source — prospects finding vendors through conversational AI, not search engines.
- Need to define what "agent adoption" means more precisely — using Gemini Deep Research counts? Or does it require deployed automation?
