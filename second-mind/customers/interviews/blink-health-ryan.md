## Interview Focus Areas

The interview is structured around understanding the ops buyer's perspective on several topics relevant to Eventual:

### Account Research Workflow
- How does the sales/marketing team build target account lists and research prospects?
- How much of that work is manual vs. enriched upstream by ops?
- What tools are in use today (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Clearbit, 6sense, LinkedIn Sales Nav)?
- Where does tooling fall short: coverage, freshness, accuracy, depth?

### Data Quality (Informed by Ataccama Experience)
- Ryan spent two years at a data management company -- he knows data quality problems that most ops people miss
- Key question: How does that experience shape how he thinks about GTM data quality?
- What are the hidden costs of bad data in the GTM stack?

### Signals and Timing
- How does the team decide which accounts to prioritize and when to engage?
- What external signals would change how the team sells if available reliably?
- Healthcare-specific signals: regulatory shifts, hiring patterns, leadership changes

### The Ops Buyer's Bar
- What makes him say "yes" to a new tool vs. "we can build this internally"?
- Static databases (ZoomInfo-style) vs. real-time signals -- does the distinction matter in practice?
- Tool adoption friction: trust, workflow friction, training, data quality

### AI in GTM Ops
- Where is AI actually changing how ops works today vs. where is it still hype?
- Biggest unlock for AI in GTM: automating internal workflows or getting better external context?

## Expected Key Themes

Based on Ryan's background and the interview questions:

1. **Data quality concerns** -- coming from Ataccama, he'll have a high bar for data accuracy and will be skeptical of tools that promise comprehensive data without demonstrating quality
2. **Tool adoption friction** -- having implemented GTM stacks at multiple companies, he knows the gap between buying a tool and getting reps to actually use it
3. **Healthcare GTM differences** -- Blink Health operates in a different GTM motion than enterprise software; account research and targeting workflows differ significantly
4. **Ops buyer perspective** -- he evaluates and selects tools, not builds them; his bar is "would I actually pay for this and can I get my team to adopt it"
