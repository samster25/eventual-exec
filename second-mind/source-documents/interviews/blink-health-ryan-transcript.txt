# Ryan — Expert Interview Questions

Background: Director, GTM Operations & Analytics at Blink Health (since May 2025).
Previously Senior Director of Global RevOps at Ataccama (data management), Senior
Director at Hypr (identity security), and Director of Global Marketing Ops at UiPath
(2018-2022). Career arc goes from marketing ops to full revenue ops. Understands
the full funnel, analytics, and has seen the ops stack at both growth-stage and
enterprise scale. Ataccama background means he knows data quality problems firsthand.

Goal: Understand how GTM ops leaders think about account research, external data,
and where the current tooling falls short. Ryan is less of an AI builder and more of
an ops buyer — he's the person who evaluates, selects, and implements GTM tools.
His perspective is "what would I actually pay for."

---

## Opening (2 min)

- Thanks for the time. We're building tools for GTM teams and trying to
  understand the workflow from the ops side before we build the wrong thing.
  Your experience across UiPath, Ataccama, and now Blink Health is exactly the
  perspective we need.

## His current role at Blink Health (10 min)

1. You joined Blink Health recently as Director of GTM Ops & Analytics. What
   does the GTM stack look like there today? What's working and what did you
   inherit that needs fixing?

2. Healthcare is a different GTM motion than enterprise software. How does the
   account research and targeting workflow differ from what you saw at Ataccama
   or UiPath?

3. What are the first things you're prioritizing in this role? Where are the
   biggest gaps?

## Account research and external context (15 min)

4. When your sales or marketing team is building target account lists or
   researching prospects — what does that process look like? Who does it and
   what tools do they use?

5. How much of that work is manual? Are reps assembling their own research
   from LinkedIn, company websites, news, etc., or is ops providing enriched
   data upstream?

6. What enrichment and intelligence tools does your team use today? (ZoomInfo,
   Apollo, Clearbit, 6sense, LinkedIn Sales Nav, etc.) What's actually useful
   vs. what's shelfware?

7. How good is the data you're getting from those tools? Where does it fall
   short — coverage, freshness, accuracy, depth?

8. You spent two years at Ataccama, which is literally a data management
   company. How does that experience shape how you think about data quality in
   the GTM stack? What problems do you see that most ops people miss?

## Signals and timing (10 min)

9. How does your team decide which accounts to prioritize and when to engage
   them? Is that intent data, scoring models, rep judgment, or something else?

10. What external signals would change how your team sells if you could get
    them reliably? (Hiring patterns, funding, leadership changes, product
    launches, regulatory shifts — especially relevant in healthcare.)

11. Is there a gap between the signals available in your current tools and the
    signals that would actually move the needle on pipeline? What's missing?

## The ops buyer perspective (10 min)

12. You've built and managed GTM stacks at multiple companies. When you evaluate
    a new tool for the stack, what's your bar? What makes you say yes vs. "we
    can build this internally"?

13. How do you think about static databases (ZoomInfo-style firmographics) vs.
    real-time signals? Does that distinction matter in practice for how your
    team operates?

14. If someone handed you a tool that could answer "tell me everything relevant
    about this company and this person right now" with real-time web data —
    where in your workflow would you plug it in first?

15. What's the hardest part of getting reps to actually use the tools and data
    that ops provides? Is it trust, workflow friction, training, data quality?

## AI in GTM ops (5 min)

16. You've seen the GTM stack evolve from UiPath through now. Where is AI
    actually changing how ops works today vs. where is it still hype?

17. Are you using or evaluating any AI-powered tools in your current stack?
    What's promising and what's been disappointing?

18. Where do you think the biggest unlock is for AI in GTM — is it automating
    internal workflows (CRM, routing, scoring) or getting better external
    context to work with?

## Close (2 min)

- Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think I should be thinking
  about?
- Anyone else you'd recommend we talk to who's working on similar problems?
