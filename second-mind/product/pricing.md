# Pricing Strategy

---

## Model: Usage-Based, Value-Tiered

Eventual uses usage-based pricing, not per-seat licensing. This aligns our revenue with the value delivered and with how agent-native products are consumed.

---

## Why Deep Research = High Usage

The core insight: deep account research generates significantly more tool calls than shallow lookup.

| Use Case | Tool Calls | Value to User |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Shallow lookup (who is the VP Sales at X?) | 1-2 calls | Low -- commodity data, available elsewhere |
| Account briefing (company profile + key people) | 5-10 calls | Medium -- saves 15-20 min of manual research |
| Deep research (full account prep with signals, org mapping, content analysis) | 15-30 calls | High -- saves 30-60 min, surfaces insights not findable elsewhere |

A user doing deep research on 10 target accounts per week generates 150-300 tool calls. This is the usage pattern we optimize for -- and it naturally self-selects for high-ACV, account-based sellers who get the most value.

---

## Value Tiers

| Tier | What It Provides | Usage Pattern |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Basic navigation** | Company search, basic profiles | Low tool call volume, commodity-level value |
| **Deep intelligence** | Full drill-down: org charts, champion identification, signal analysis | Medium-high volume, differentiated value |
| **Premium signals** | Watch/monitor, proactive alerts, timing signals, content intelligence | Ongoing usage, highest value -- signals come to you |

Pricing scales with the tier of value consumed, not with the number of humans on the team.

---

## Why Not Per-Seat

Per-seat pricing is a legacy model that penalizes adoption and misaligns incentives:

1. **Agent-native products don't have "seats."** The user might be one GTM engineer running agents that serve an entire sales team. Per-seat doesn't capture this.
2. **Penalizes sharing.** If one AE finds value and wants to share with the team, per-seat creates friction. Usage-based encourages expansion.
3. **Misaligned with value.** A power user doing deep research on 50 accounts/week gets 10x more value than a casual user. Per-seat charges them the same.
4. **ZoomInfo's model is dying.** ZoomInfo ($1.25B rev, 1-3% growth) is per-seat with bulk export. The market is moving away from this model.

---

## Early Adopter Strategy

**First 10 users are free or heavily discounted.** We are optimizing for PMF, not revenue, for the next 8 weeks. PMF means 10 people who would be genuinely unhappy if we took the tool away.

- Weeks 1-8: Free access for first 10 users
- Weeks 11-12 (if hypotheses confirmed): First pricing experiments with early adopter discounts
- Revenue is a signal, not a target, in this phase

---

## Pricing Experiments (Weeks 11-12, Post-Hypothesis Confirmation)

If H1 + H2 + H3 are confirmed:

- Test willingness to pay at different usage levels
- Test value perception: do users see deep research as worth 10x shallow lookup?
- Test packaging: bundled plans vs. pure usage-based vs. hybrid
- Gather data on usage patterns to calibrate tiers

The goal is revenue signal -- understanding price sensitivity and value perception -- not revenue optimization. Optimization comes later with more users and more data.

---

## Competitive Pricing Context

| Competitor | Pricing Model | Our Advantage |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **ZoomInfo** | Per-seat, $15K-$50K+/year per team | Usage-based scales with value, not headcount |
| **Clay** | Per-seat + credits, $150-$800/mo | We provide intelligence, not orchestration. Different value prop. |
| **Apollo** | Per-seat, freemium | We go deep on fewer accounts rather than broad on many |
| **Exa** | API usage-based | We provide contextualized intelligence, not raw search results |

Our pricing should reflect that we solve a fundamentally different problem: deep account preparation for high-ACV sales, not contact list building.
